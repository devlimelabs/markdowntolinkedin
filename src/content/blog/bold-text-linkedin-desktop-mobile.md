## Why Doesn't LinkedIn Have a Bold Button?

It seems like a basic feature, right? The simple reason is that LinkedIn‚Äôs post feed is designed for plain text. Unlike LinkedIn Articles, which have a full formatting toolbar, posts are meant to be simple and uniform. This ensures a consistent look across the millions of posts shared daily.

This limitation forces users to get creative. The most common solution is using Unicode, a standard that assigns a unique number to every character, including different styles of letters and numbers.

## A Word of Caution: The Downsides of Unicode

Using Unicode for bold text is a great trick, but it has a couple of important drawbacks you should know about.

*   **Accessibility Issues:** Screen readers, which are used by visually impaired individuals, often can't read Unicode characters correctly. They might read the text letter by letter or simply announce it as a series of unknown symbols. This makes your content inaccessible to a part of your audience.
*   **Search Problems:** LinkedIn‚Äôs search function may not recognize Unicode characters as standard text. If you bold an important keyword, your post might not show up in search results for that term. It's best to avoid using it for anything critical for SEO.

Because of this, you should use bold formatting for emphasis, not for essential information. Use it to highlight a feeling, a key result, or a call-to-action. Don't use it for names, companies, or keywords you want people to find.
