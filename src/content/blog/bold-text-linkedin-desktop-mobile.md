### Why Should You Use Bold Text in Your Posts?

Formatting isn't just about looking fancy. It’s about communication. On a fast-scrolling feed, bold text grabs attention and makes your message easier to understand. Here’s why it’s a smart move:

*   **Improves Readability:** Breaking up text with bold headings and keywords helps readers scan your content and find the most important information quickly. With over 70% of LinkedIn users on mobile, scannable content is essential.
*   **Creates Emphasis:** You can guide your reader’s attention to a key takeaway, a call-to-action, or a powerful statement. It’s the digital equivalent of raising your voice for effect.
*   **Boosts Engagement:** A well-formatted post is more inviting to read. When people read your post, they are more likely to comment and react. One simple A/B test showed that posts with bold text received 60% more comments than plain ones.

### The Universal Method for Bolding Text

Since LinkedIn doesn’t have a built-in formatting toolbar for posts, you have to use an external tool. The process works the same way whether you are on a desktop or a mobile device because it all happens in a web browser.

Here is the simple four-step process.

![A 4-step infographic showing how to bold text on LinkedIn: Write Text, Use a Formatter Tool, Copy Formatted Text, and Paste into LinkedIn.](/blog/images/linkedin-bold-text-process.jpg)

1.  **Write Your Text:** Draft your post in a notes app, a text editor, or directly in the formatter tool.
2.  **Use a Formatter Tool:** Paste your text into a Unicode or Markdown text formatter.
3.  **Copy the Formatted Text:** The tool will generate text that looks bold. Copy this new version.
4.  **Paste into LinkedIn:** Paste the copied text directly into the LinkedIn post editor.

That’s it. What you see is what you get.

### Method 2: The Manual Way with a Unicode Generator

If you only need to bold a word or two, a simple Unicode text generator works just fine. These websites take your plain text and convert it into thousands of different Unicode characters, including many that look like bold letters.

Popular options include YayText and LingoJam.

**How it works on Desktop or Mobile:**

1.  Open a tool like YayText in your browser.
2.  Type the text you want to format.
3.  Scroll through the options and click "Copy" next to the bold style you like.
4.  Paste it into your LinkedIn post.

This method is simple but can be slow if you have a lot of text to format.

### A Quick Word of Warning: Accessibility Matters

It's important to know that this "bold" text isn't technically formatting. It's a series of special Unicode characters. For most people, it looks like bold text. But for visually impaired users who rely on screen readers, it can be a problem. A screen reader might spell out the characters one by one or announce them as symbols, making your post difficult to understand.

**Best Practice:** Use bold formatting for emphasis, but don't place your most critical keywords or hashtags in Unicode. Keep the essential message in plain text to ensure everyone can read it.

### Desktop vs. Mobile: Is There Really a Difference?

No. The process for making text bold is **exactly the same** on both desktop and mobile.

Because the formatting happens in a web-based tool, it doesn’t matter what device you use. You can write a post on your computer, format it, and paste it into the LinkedIn website. Or, you can do the entire process on your phone's web browser and paste it into the LinkedIn mobile app.

The result is identical.
