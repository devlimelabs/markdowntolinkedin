Think of formatting as the body language of your writing. Here's how to make it work:

### 1. Master the Hook (First 2 Lines)

LinkedIn hides content after two lines behind "...see more." Those first two lines are your entire sales pitch.

**‚ùå Wrong:** "I've been thinking a lot about leadership lately and wanted to share some thoughts."

## Critical Mistakes That Kill Your Reach

Avoid these formatting traps that signal low-quality content:

### The Algorithm Killers

**1. Wall of Text Syndrome**
- Long paragraphs are the fastest way to lose readers
- Break every 1-2 lines for mobile readability

**2. Link Placement Error**  
- Never put external links directly in posts
- LinkedIn's algorithm suppresses posts with external links
- Always put links in the first comment

**3. Hashtag Overload**
- Stick to 3-5 highly relevant hashtags
- Place them at the end, not scattered throughout
- Research hashtag popularity before using

**4. Over-Formatting Chaos**
- Don't use ALL CAPS for entire sentences
- Avoid excessive bold or Unicode characters
- When everything is emphasized, nothing is

### The Accessibility Fails

**Screen Reader Issues:**
- Unicode characters may be read as symbol names
- Excessive emojis create noise for assistive technology  
- Missing context for visual elements

**Device Compatibility:**
- Some Android devices can't render all Unicode
- Always test on different devices when possible
- Have a plain-text backup plan
