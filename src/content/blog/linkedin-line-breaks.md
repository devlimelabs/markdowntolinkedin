## Why Does LinkedIn Remove Line Breaks?

LinkedIn’s post editor isn’t like a normal word processor. It’s designed to handle simple text and has a few quirks in how it processes formatting. The main issue is how it treats “empty” lines.

When you press `Enter` once, you create what looks like a new line. But if that line contains no characters, LinkedIn’s rendering engine often ignores it when displaying the post to other people. It sees an empty line as unnecessary and collapses it to save space, which is what turns your structured post into a single block of text.

This problem is especially noticeable when moving between desktop and mobile. A post written on a desktop computer might look fine to you, but appear as a wall of text to someone scrolling on their phone. With over 80% of engagement happening on mobile, getting your spacing right is critical.

## How to Add Line Breaks That Actually Work

Here are a few proven methods to add and preserve line breaks in your LinkedIn posts, from the simplest to the most technical.

![Four methods to add line breaks in LinkedIn posts](/blog/images/linkedin-line-breaks-methods.png)

### 1. The Double Enter Method

The easiest and most common way to create a clean paragraph break is to press `Enter` twice. This creates a full empty line between your paragraphs, which LinkedIn is more likely to respect and render correctly.

*   **For a standard paragraph break:** Press `Enter` twice.
*   **For a simple new line (within the same thought):** Pressing `Enter` once can work, but it’s less reliable.

This method is a good starting point and works for most simple posts.

### 2. Edit on the Mobile App

For reasons known only to LinkedIn’s engineers, editing and posting from the mobile app often produces more reliable formatting. Many users have found that if their line breaks are disappearing when posting from a desktop, pasting the same text into the LinkedIn mobile app and posting from there solves the problem.

If you’re struggling with formatting, try this workflow:

1.  Write your post in a notes app or on your computer.
2.  Copy the text.
3.  Paste it into the “Start a post” field on the LinkedIn mobile app.
4.  Make any final adjustments and post.

This seems to force LinkedIn to preserve the line breaks across all devices.

### 3. The Invisible Character Trick

If you absolutely need an empty line and the double enter method isn’t working, you can use a more advanced trick: the invisible character.

This involves placing a special Unicode character that is invisible to the human eye onto the empty line. Because the line is no longer technically “empty,” LinkedIn is forced to render it.

Here’s how to do it:

1.  Copy the invisible character between these brackets: `[⠀]`
2.  In your LinkedIn post, create a new line where you want the break.
3.  Paste the invisible character onto that line.

This is a solid workaround for stubborn formatting bugs, especially in the “About” section of your profile, which is known to have similar issues.

## Best Practices for Using Line Breaks

Adding line breaks isn’t just about preventing walls of text. It’s about making your content scannable and engaging.

*   **Keep Paragraphs Short:** Aim for 1-3 sentences per paragraph. This makes your content much easier to read on a small mobile screen.
*   **Use White Space Strategically:** A line break can add a dramatic pause or emphasize a key point. Use extra space to guide the reader’s eye down the page.
*   **Hook the Reader:** Your first two lines are the most important. Use them to present a bold claim or ask an interesting question to get people to click “see more.”

By mastering line breaks, you can create LinkedIn content that is not only readable but also more engaging and professional.
