## Why Does LinkedIn Keep Removing Your Line Breaks?

Understanding the "why" helps you prevent the problem from ever happening again. It’s not a single bug, but a few quirks in how LinkedIn handles text.

### 1. The Hidden Problem: Invisible Formatting

When you copy text from another source like Google Docs, Microsoft Word, or even an email, you’re also copying invisible formatting code. Things like non-breaking spaces, soft returns, and other background data get pasted into LinkedIn’s editor.

LinkedIn’s editor tries to interpret this hidden code, and often, it gets confused. In its confusion, it defaults to the safest option: stripping out all the formatting, which includes your precious line breaks. The result is a dense, unreadable block of text.

### 2. The Copy-Paste Trap

This is the most common cause of formatting collapse. You write your post in a separate app, paste it into LinkedIn, and the spacing disappears. The plain text reset method mentioned above is the best way to avoid this trap. It cleanses your text of any troublesome invisible code before you paste it into LinkedIn.

### 3. Desktop vs. Mobile App Inconsistencies

Have you ever noticed that a post looks fine on your computer but is a mess on your phone? You’re not imagining it. The desktop and mobile versions of LinkedIn use different editors. Content formatted on one device can sometimes render incorrectly on the other.

As a general rule, the desktop editor is more reliable. If you’re crafting an important post, it’s always best to do it on a computer.

## How to Reliably Add Line Breaks (3 Simple Methods)

Now for the solutions. These methods will help you preserve your formatting and keep your posts looking clean and professional.

### Method 1: The Plain Text Paste (The Ultimate Fix)

This is the most foolproof way to solve formatting issues. It works by stripping away all the invisible code that causes problems.

1.  **Write your post** in any external editor you like.
2.  **Copy your full text.**
3.  **Paste it into a plain text editor.**
    *   On Windows, use **Notepad**.
    *   On Mac, use **TextEdit** (make sure it’s in plain text mode by going to `Format > Make Plain Text`).
4.  **Copy the text again** from the plain text editor.
5.  **Paste it into LinkedIn.**

This simple two-step copy-paste process cleanses the text, leaving you with just the words and basic spacing. You can then re-apply any bold or italic formatting directly within LinkedIn with much less risk.

### Method 2: Use Double Line Breaks

When writing directly in LinkedIn or after pasting your clean text, always use double line breaks between paragraphs. This means pressing the `Enter` key twice.

*   **A single `Enter`** creates a line break, which can sometimes be ignored.
*   **A double `Enter`** creates a new paragraph, which is much more stable and consistently rendered across devices.

This creates essential white space that makes your content scannable and easier on the eyes. It’s a small habit that makes a huge difference.
