## Why Does LinkedIn Remove Line Breaks?

LinkedIn’s post editor is designed to be a simple, plain-text-first environment. It doesn’t have the rich formatting options of a word processor like Microsoft Word or Google Docs. When you copy and paste text from these sources, you’re also copying hidden formatting code in the background. This can include things like font styles, colors, and, most importantly, different types of line and paragraph breaks.

LinkedIn’s editor tries to interpret this hidden code, but often gets it wrong. It might strip out all the spacing, resulting in a single block of text. This is especially common when pasting content from other websites or complex documents. Writing in a plain text editor or a Markdown-based tool helps avoid this problem by ensuring you’re only working with clean, unformatted text.

## How to Add Line Breaks That Actually Work

Let’s dig into the practical methods for getting your line breaks to behave. These techniques range from simple keyboard tricks to using external tools for a perfect post every time.

### 1. The Double `Enter` Method (Desktop)

This is the most straightforward approach. When writing a post directly in the LinkedIn desktop interface, simply press the `Enter` key twice to create a paragraph break. This inserts a blank line between your paragraphs, which is the most common and readable way to space out your content.

**Example:**

Your first paragraph goes here.

(Press `Enter` twice)

Your second paragraph starts on a new line, with a clean space above it.

This method is perfect for most text-only posts. It’s simple, fast, and reliable for content written directly on the platform.

### 2. Write in a Plain Text Editor First

This is a professional’s secret for consistent formatting. Instead of writing directly in LinkedIn, compose your post in a plain text editor. This ensures there’s no hidden formatting to mess things up.

*   **Windows:** Use Notepad.
*   **Mac:** Use TextEdit (make sure it’s in plain text mode: `Format > Make Plain Text`).
*   **Any Platform:** Use an online tool like the [MarkdownToLinkedIn editor](/), which is designed specifically for this purpose.

Once you’ve written your post in one of these tools, you can confidently copy and paste it into LinkedIn. The line breaks you added will be preserved.

### 3. The Mobile App Advantage

For reasons that aren’t entirely clear, the LinkedIn mobile app often handles line breaks more gracefully than the desktop site. If you’re having trouble with a post’s formatting, try editing it on your phone.

You can either write the post from scratch in the app or copy and paste your text into the mobile editor and adjust the spacing there. Many users find that line breaks created on mobile are more likely to stick, especially for more complex posts.

## Best Practices for Using Line Breaks

Adding line breaks is just the first step. Using them effectively is what makes your content truly readable and engaging.

![LinkedIn post formatting comparison showing before and after examples](/blog/images/linkedin-formatting-comparison.jpg)

*   **Keep Paragraphs Short:** Aim for paragraphs that are no more than 2-3 lines long. This creates a lot of white space and makes your post feel less intimidating to read.
*   **Use One-Line Paragraphs for Emphasis:** A single, powerful sentence on its own line can have a huge impact. It forces the reader to pause and consider the point you’re making.
*   **Create Lists with Line Breaks:** Use line breaks to separate items in a list, especially when using bullet points or emojis. This makes the list scannable and easy to digest.

**Example of a well-formatted list:**

Here are my top three tips for better LinkedIn posts:

✅ Write a strong hook.

✅ Keep your paragraphs short.

✅ End with a clear question.
