## The Manual Way: Using Unicode Text Generators

The most common DIY method for bolding text on LinkedIn involves using Unicode characters. Unicode is a standard that assigns a unique number to every character, symbol, and emoji. It includes a set of characters that look like bold letters.

Here’s how you do it:

1.  **Find a Unicode Text Generator:** Search for a tool like YayText or LingoJam.
2.  **Type Your Text:** Enter the text you want to make bold.
3.  **Copy the Bold Version:** The tool will generate your text in various styles. Copy the bold version.
4.  **Paste into LinkedIn:** Paste the copied text directly into your LinkedIn post.

It seems simple, right? But there are some serious downsides to this method.

### The Problems with Unicode Bold Text

Using Unicode characters for bolding isn't true formatting. You're just using a different set of characters that happen to look bold. This can cause a few problems.

First, **screen readers can't read them**. This makes your content inaccessible to visually impaired users. Instead of hearing the bolded word, they might hear a string of confusing character names or just silence.

Second, **LinkedIn's search won't find them**. If you put an important keyword in Unicode bold, your post won't show up when people search for that keyword. You're actually hurting your post's visibility.

Finally, these characters can sometimes appear as empty boxes or weird symbols on older devices, especially on Android. It’s a risk that can make your post look broken to a part of your audience.
