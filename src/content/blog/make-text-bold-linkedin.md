Ever wonder how people make their text **bold** on LinkedIn? It’s a great way to make your posts pop and grab attention. But here’s the thing: LinkedIn doesn’t have a built-in button for it. So, how’s it done?

You don't actually need fancy tools to do it. You can format your text right from your computer or phone. This guide will show you how to do it manually, so you can add some punch to your posts whenever you want.

## TL;DR: 3 Ways to Make Text Bold on LinkedIn

| Method | Description | Best For |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Manual Unicode** | Copying and pasting special Unicode characters that look like bold text. | Quick, one-off bolding without any external tools. |
| **Markdown Conversion** | Writing in Markdown (`**bold**`) and using a converter. | Developers or writers who prefer Markdown for everything. |

## Why Bother with Bold Text on LinkedIn?

In a sea of text, bold formatting helps you stand out. It’s perfect for:

*   **Highlighting key points:** Make sure your most important messages get read.
*   **Creating structure:** Use bold text for unofficial headlines to break up long posts.
*   **Drawing attention:** A little bit of bold can make your entire post more visually appealing.

But remember, with great power comes great responsibility. Overusing bold text can make your post look spammy and hard to read. Use it strategically.

## The Manual Method: Unicode Characters

This is the “no tools required” approach. It works because you’re not actually using a special font. You’re using different characters that just *look* like bold letters. These are part of the Unicode standard, which is why they work on LinkedIn and other platforms.

### How to Do It:

1.  **Find a Unicode character map or list.** Your computer has one built-in (Character Map on Windows, Character Viewer on Mac). You can also search online for “Unicode bold characters.”
2.  **Copy the bold character you need.** For example, to get a bold ‘A’, you’d find and copy **A** (U+1D400).
3.  **Paste it into your LinkedIn post.**

It’s that simple. But, it’s also a bit tedious to do for more than a few words. You have to find and copy each letter one by one.

### The Downside of Unicode

There are a few things you should know before you go all-in on Unicode bolding:

*   **Accessibility:** Screen readers, which are used by people with visual impairments, might not read these characters correctly. They might read them out as individual characters or just say “symbol.”
*   **Search:** LinkedIn’s search might not recognize Unicode bold text. So, don’t put your main keywords in bold.
*   **Platform issues:** On some older devices, these characters might show up as empty boxes.

So, while it’s a neat trick, it’s best to use it for emphasis, not for critical information.

## Best Practices for Using Bold Text

Ready to start bolding? Here are a few tips to keep in mind:

*   **Don’t overdo it.** A little bold goes a long way. Too much, and your post becomes hard to read.
*   **Use it for emphasis.** Bold text is great for highlighting a key statistic, a powerful quote, or a call to action.
*   **Create structure.** Use bold for subheadings in longer posts to make them easier to scan.
*   **Mix it up.** Combine bold with italics to create even more visual interest.
