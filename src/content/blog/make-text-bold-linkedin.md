You've seen it. That bold, eye-catching text in a LinkedIn post that stops your scroll. You try hitting Ctrl+B, but nothing happens. So, how do they do it?

It’s a common frustration. You can’t format text directly on LinkedIn, but people are clearly making it happen. The secret isn't a hidden feature; it's a clever workaround using special characters. And you don't need to be a tech wizard or use a fancy tool to do it yourself.

This guide will show you exactly how to create bold text manually for your LinkedIn posts. We'll also cover the significant downsides of this method and introduce a much simpler approach for anyone who values their time.

## Why Can't You Just... Make Text Bold?

LinkedIn keeps its platform's text plain and simple for a reason: consistency. They want to ensure that every post looks the same across countless devices, from the newest iPhone to an old Android tablet or a desktop browser. By stripping away native formatting options like bold, italics, or different fonts, they create a uniform reading experience for everyone.

But what you're seeing isn't actually bold text in the way a word processor creates it. It's an illusion created with **Unicode characters**. These are special symbols from a massive character set that includes mathematical symbols, symbols from other languages, and, importantly, characters that *look* like bold letters. To LinkedIn's system, you're not styling the letter 'A'; you're using a completely different character that just happens to look like a bold 'A'.

## The Manual Method: Your DIY Guide to Bold Text

Ready to try it yourself? You don't need to download anything. All you need is your web browser.

Here’s the step-by-step process:

1.  **Find a Unicode Text Generator:** Open your browser and search for a "Unicode text generator" or "bold text generator." You'll find dozens of free websites that do the same thing.
2.  **Type Your Text:** In the generator's input box, type the word or phrase you want to make bold.
3.  **Copy the Bold Version:** The tool will instantly show your text in various styles. Find the simple bold (usually called "Bold Sans" or "Bold Serif") and click the copy button.
4.  **Paste it into LinkedIn:** Go back to your LinkedIn post, profile, or comment and simply paste the text. It will appear bold.

That's it. You've manually created bold text on LinkedIn. You can use this method for headlines, to emphasize key points in a list, or to make a call-to-action stand out.

## The Big Problems with Manual Bold Text

While the manual method works, it comes with some serious drawbacks you need to be aware of. It's not just about the extra effort.

First, **it's terrible for accessibility**. As John Espirian, a well-known LinkedIn expert, points out, screen readers for visually impaired users often can't interpret these special characters correctly. Instead of reading your bold text, the software might read out a confusing jumble of character names or simply skip it. This makes your content inaccessible to a portion of your audience.

Second, **it hurts your search visibility**. The LinkedIn algorithm doesn't see these Unicode symbols as regular text. So, if you make a keyword bold, you're effectively hiding it from LinkedIn's search function. People searching for that term won't find your post. Research from various SEO and social media experts confirms that you should always keep your most important keywords in plain text.

Finally, it can sometimes fail on older devices, showing up as empty squares (☐☐☐) instead of your intended text.

## A Better Way: The Markdown Method

So, the manual method is a bit of a hassle and has some major flaws. What's the alternative?

For anyone who writes content regularly, the answer is **Markdown**. It's a simple syntax for formatting text that's easy to learn and incredibly efficient. To make text bold in Markdown, you just wrap it in two asterisks, like this: `**This is bold text**`.

Here’s the workflow:

1.  Write your entire LinkedIn post in a plain text editor using Markdown for all your formatting (bold, italics, bullet points).
2.  Copy your Markdown text.
3.  Paste it into a **Markdown-to-LinkedIn converter** (like the one on our homepage!).

The tool instantly converts your Markdown into the correct Unicode characters for LinkedIn, ready for you to copy and paste. It's faster, more consistent, and lets you focus on writing, not on finding and copying special characters. It's the workflow that developers and writers have been using for years to be more efficient.

Making your LinkedIn posts stand out is important, but doing it the right way matters. While you can manually create bold text without any special tools, the accessibility and SEO problems make it a poor choice for important content. Give the Markdown method a try; it's a small change to your workflow that saves a lot of time and produces much better results.
