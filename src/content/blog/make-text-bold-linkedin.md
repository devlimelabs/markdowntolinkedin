Ever see a LinkedIn post with **bold text** and wonder how they did it? You're not alone. LinkedIn doesn't have a built-in button for bolding text in posts, but people do it all the time. It’s a great way to make key points pop and guide your reader’s eye.

You don't need fancy tools to get it done. You can do it manually, and it only takes a few seconds. Plus, there's an even easier way if you're a fan of writing in markdown.

Let's look at how to make your text bold on LinkedIn, first the manual way, and then a simpler workflow.

## TL;DR: The Quick Answer

| Method | How It Works | Best For |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Manual (Unicode)** | Copy-paste text into a Unicode generator, then copy the bold version into LinkedIn. | Quick, one-off bolding when you don't have any tools handy. |
| **Markdown Converter** | Write your post using markdown's `**bold**` syntax, then convert it. | A faster, more natural workflow, especially for developers or writers. |

## The Manual Method: Using Unicode Characters

The secret to bold text on LinkedIn isn't a hidden feature. It's Unicode. Unicode is a standard that gives every character a unique number, including special versions of letters that look bold.

So, when you see bold text on LinkedIn, you're not seeing formatted text. You're seeing a different set of characters altogether.

![Unicode Bold Text Conversion Process](/blog/images/unicode-bold-text-process.png)

Here's how to do it yourself without any special software:

1.  **Find a Unicode Text Generator:** Search for "bold text generator" or "Unicode text converter." You'll find plenty of free web-based tools like YayText or Capitalize My Title.
2.  **Type Your Text:** Enter the word or phrase you want to make bold into the tool.
3.  **Copy the Bold Version:** The tool will generate several font styles. Find the simple bold version (it usually looks like **this**) and copy it.
4.  **Paste into LinkedIn:** Paste the copied text directly into your LinkedIn post, comment, or even your profile headline.

That's it. You've manually created bold text. It’s a simple copy-and-paste job.

### But Wait. There Are Downsides.

This Unicode trick is clever, but it has some serious drawbacks you need to know about. It’s not a perfect solution.

**1. It's Bad for Accessibility.**
Screen readers, which are used by visually impaired people, often can't read these special Unicode characters correctly. They might read them out as individual characters, a string of symbols, or just skip them entirely. If your bolded text is important, some of your audience will miss it.

**2. It's Not Searchable.**
LinkedIn's search function doesn't recognize these Unicode characters as standard text. If you write a post with the word "**important**" in Unicode bold, and someone searches for "important," your post won't show up. This is a big problem if you're trying to get found for specific keywords.

**3. It Can Look Weird on Some Devices.**
On older devices, especially some Android phones, these characters might not render at all. Instead, you'll see empty boxes (we call them 'tofu'). While this is becoming less common, it's still a risk.

So, what's a better way?

## A Simpler Method: Write in Markdown

If you find yourself wanting to bold text often, the manual copy-paste dance gets old. A much smoother workflow is to write your posts in markdown first.

Markdown is a simple way to format text using plain characters. You've probably seen it before. To make text bold in markdown, you just wrap it in two asterisks, like this: `**your bold text**`.

This is a lot more natural to write. You don't have to leave your editor, go to a website, and copy-paste. You just type.

Once you've written your post in markdown, you can use a tool like [MarkdownToLinkedIn.com](https://markdowntolinkedin.com/) to instantly convert it. It takes your `**bold text**` and turns it into the correct Unicode characters for LinkedIn, along with handling italics, bullet points, and other formatting.

This gives you the best of both worlds: a simple, efficient writing process and the formatted output you want for LinkedIn.

## When Should You Use Bold Text?

Given the downsides, you should be thoughtful about how you use bold text. It's a powerful tool for emphasis, but it's not for everything.

Here are some good times to use it:

*   **To Highlight Key Takeaways:** Make it easy for skimmers to get the main points of your post.
*   **For Section Headers:** Break up a long post into smaller, more readable chunks.
*   **To Emphasize a Single Word:** Add a little punch to a sentence.

Just remember the golden rule: **if the text is essential, don't put it in Unicode bold.** Keep your most important keywords and messages in plain text to make sure everyone can read and find them.
